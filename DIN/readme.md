
GARCH model
--
In my research on the August 2024 volatility regime, I employed a GARCH(1,1) model to analyze the 'identity crisis' Gold faced during the August 5th deleveraging event. By isolating the conditional variance, I found a remarkably high persistence coefficient of 0.90, which suggests that the market’s uncertainty wasn't just a one-day reaction to the NFP news. Instead, the data reveals a significant volatility cluster where the asset was caught in a 'dual-interpretation' trap—simultaneously being sought as a safe haven while being sold for global liquidity. My findings, prove that the Gold market requires a prolonged period to digest macro-shocks of this nature, moving from an unstable Log-Likelihood state back to an efficient equilibrium only by early September.
