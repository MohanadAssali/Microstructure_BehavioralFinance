### Note
This research comes mainly from my own hands-on watching of financial markets over the past five years, plus the time I spent manually going through and classifying more than a thousand macroeconomic news events. I did use some quantitative tools like GARCH models and Monte Carlo simulations to test and support parts of the analysis, but I didn't lean on historical data as the main way to forecast anything. I'm guided by the spirit of the Lucas Critique (from Robert Lucas back in 1976). Markets aren't static — people adapt, policies shift, and behavior changes in ways that make old statistical relationships unreliable. That's why I put more weight on qualitative judgment and understanding the specific context of each situation, rather than trying to force everything into purely data-driven models. In the end, I think this approach does a better job of capturing how messy and adaptive financial markets really are
